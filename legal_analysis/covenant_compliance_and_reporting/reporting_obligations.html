Typical Reporting Obligations under a Leveraged Loan Agreement

Leveraged loan agreements impose various reporting obligations on the borrower, primarily through affirmative covenants (often found in a section titled "Information Undertakings"). These undertakings ensure that lenders (via the Facility Agent) receive timely and accurate information to monitor the borrower's financial health, operational performance, and compliance with the terms of the loan. This page outlines common reporting requirements, drawing from Section II.D.1 (Affirmative Covenants - Information Undertakings) of the "Leveraged Lending: A Comprehensive Legal Knowledge Base" (Source Document).

Source Document: Section II.D.1, Table 2

I. Financial Reporting

This is the cornerstone of information undertakings, providing lenders with regular updates on the borrower's financial performance and condition.

Report Type Typical Frequency Typical Due Date (Post Period-End) Common Details & Requirements (based on Source Document: Section II.D.1, [6])
Annual Financial Statements Annually 90-120 days (negotiable, may be longer for public companies)
  • Consolidated balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement for the borrower and its restricted subsidiaries (the "Group").
  • Must be **audited** by a reputable independent accounting firm acceptable to the lenders (or Agent).
  • Prepared in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) or International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS), applied consistently with past practice (unless changes are disclosed and explained).
  • Auditor's report should ideally be unqualified (a "clean" opinion). An audit qualification, especially regarding "going concern" (unless related to an upcoming maturity of the loan itself which is planned to be refinanced), can be an Event of Default (Source Document: Section II.E).
Quarterly (or Semi-Annual) Financial Statements Quarterly (most common for leveraged loans) or Semi-Annually 45-60 days (negotiable)
  • Consolidated balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement for the Group.
  • Usually **unaudited** (management accounts), but must be certified by an authorized officer (e.g., CFO) as fairly presenting the financial condition and results of operations, subject to year-end audit adjustments and absence of footnotes.
  • Prepared in accordance with GAAP/IFRS, consistently applied.
  • Often require comparison against the budget for the current fiscal year and against the corresponding period of the previous fiscal year.
Compliance Certificate Delivered concurrently with annual and quarterly/semi-annual financial statements. Same as accompanying financials.
  • Signed by an authorized officer of the borrower (e.g., CFO, Treasurer).
  • Certifies whether the borrower is in compliance with its **financial covenants** for the relevant test period, showing detailed calculations for each covenant, referencing the defined terms in the credit agreement.
  • States whether any **Default or Event of Default** has occurred and is continuing. If so, provides specific details of the event and any remedial action being taken or proposed.
  • May also require certification of compliance with other key undertakings or confirmation of the list of guarantors and restricted/unrestricted subsidiaries.

Source Document: Section II.D.1, [6]
Annual Budget / Financial Projections Annually Typically 30-60 days before the start of the borrower's next fiscal year (or as otherwise agreed).
  • Detailed consolidated financial projections for the upcoming fiscal year (and sometimes for a longer period, e.g., 3-5 years, on a summary basis).
  • Usually includes projected income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement, often on a quarterly or monthly basis for the first year.
  • Serves as a basis for comparing actual performance and for lenders to assess future viability and debt service capacity.

II. Non-Financial Reporting & Notices (Information Undertakings)

Beyond regular financial statements, borrowers are typically required by affirmative covenants to provide other information and notices promptly or upon request:

  • Notice of Default or Event of Default: The borrower must promptly (e.g., within 1-3 business days of an authorized officer becoming aware) notify the Facility Agent of the occurrence of any Default or Event of Default, providing a statement specifying the nature of the Default/EoD and any steps being taken to remedy it. (Source Document: Section II.D.1)
  • Notice of Material Litigation: Prompt notification of the commencement or any material development in any litigation, arbitration, or administrative proceedings which are current, pending, or (to its knowledge) threatened against any member of the Group, if such proceedings could reasonably be expected to have a Material Adverse Effect (MAE). (Source Document: Section II.C - Representation on Litigation; Section II.D.1 implies such notices)
  • Notice of Material Adverse Effect (MAE): Some agreements may require specific notification if any event or circumstance occurs that has resulted in or could reasonably be expected to result in an MAE (as defined in the agreement).
  • Information for "Know Your Customer" (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Purposes: An ongoing obligation to provide information and documentation reasonably requested by any lender or the Agent to enable them to comply with their internal KYC policies and applicable AML laws and regulations. This can include information about ownership structure, beneficial owners, directors, etc.
  • Information on Security/Guarantees: Notices regarding changes to guarantors (e.g., accession of new guarantors if required by a coverage test), issues with perfection of security, or other matters affecting the security package.
  • Shareholder and Other Public Information: Copies of documents dispatched by the borrower to its shareholders generally (or filed with securities exchanges or regulators) often need to be provided to the Agent.
  • Insurance Information: Details of insurance policies maintained, evidence of renewal, and notification of material claims.
  • Other Information: A general catch-all provision requiring the borrower to provide such other information concerning its business, financial condition, assets, operations, or compliance with the terms of the loan documents as the Facility Agent or any Lender (sometimes only through the Agent) may from time to time reasonably request.

III. Access Rights (Affirmative Covenant)

  • Access to Books, Records, and Properties: Lenders (usually acting through the Facility Agent and sometimes with their own representatives) often have the right, upon reasonable prior notice and during normal business hours, to:
    • Visit the borrower's (and material subsidiaries') premises.
    • Inspect its properties.
    • Examine its books and records (and take copies or extracts).
    • Discuss its affairs, finances, and accounts with its officers and (sometimes, with borrower consent or presence) its independent auditors.
    The frequency of such visits may be limited (e.g., once per year) unless a Default or Event of Default is continuing, in which case more frequent access is usually permitted.

    • Source Document: Section II.D.1

Important Considerations: Failure to comply with these reporting obligations in a timely and accurate manner can itself constitute an Event of Default under the credit agreement. The specific scope, timing, content, and format of reporting are detailed in the affirmative covenants section (often titled "Information Undertakings" or similar) of the credit agreement and are subject to negotiation. For lenders, diligent review of this information is a key part of ongoing credit monitoring.
