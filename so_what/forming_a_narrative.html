Forming a Narrative

Structuring a Compelling Financial Narrative

A financial narrative is more than just a collection of facts and figures. It's a story that explains what the numbers mean and why they matter. A well-structured narrative can make complex financial information easier to understand and more persuasive.

1. Identify the Key Message

Before you start writing, you need to know what you want to say. What is the single most important takeaway from your analysis? This should be a clear and concise statement that summarizes your main point.

2. Structure the Story

A good story has a beginning, a middle, and an end. In a financial narrative, this translates to:

  • The Hook: Start with a compelling fact or question that grabs the reader's attention.
  • The Body: This is where you present your analysis and evidence. Use a logical flow that builds your case and leads the reader to your conclusion.
  • The Conclusion: Summarize your key findings and reiterate your main message. This is also where you should answer the "so what?" question.

3. Use Data to Support the Narrative

Data is the backbone of any financial narrative. But it's not enough to just present the data. You need to explain what it means and how it supports your story. Use charts and graphs to visualize the data and make it easier to understand.
