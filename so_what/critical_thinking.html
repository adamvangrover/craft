Critical Thinking

A Framework for Critical Thinking in Financial Analysis

Critical thinking is the ability to analyze information objectively and make a reasoned judgment. It's an essential skill for any financial analyst.

1. Identify Biases

We all have biases that can cloud our judgment. The first step in critical thinking is to be aware of your own biases and take steps to mitigate them.

2. Evaluate Assumptions

Every analysis is based on a set of assumptions. It's important to evaluate these assumptions and consider how they might affect your conclusions.

3. Draw Logical Conclusions

Your conclusions should be based on a logical analysis of the evidence. Avoid making leaps of faith or drawing conclusions that are not supported by the data.
