Iterating on Reports

How to Iterate on Your Reports to Make Them More Effective

Your reports are a reflection of your work. It's important to make sure that they're clear, concise, and persuasive. The best way to do this is to iterate on them over time.

How to Get Feedback

  • Ask for feedback from your colleagues: Your colleagues can be a great source of feedback. Ask them to review your reports and to give you their honest opinion.
  • Ask for feedback from your manager: Your manager can also be a great source of feedback. Ask them for their input on your reports.
  • Ask for feedback from your clients: If you're writing reports for clients, ask them for their feedback. This will help you to make sure that your reports are meeting their needs.

How to Incorporate Feedback

  • Be open to feedback: Be open to feedback, even if it's critical. The goal is to make your reports better.
  • Don't get defensive: Don't get defensive. Thank the person for their feedback and let them know that you'll take it into consideration.
  • Make changes as needed: Make changes to your reports as needed. The goal is to make them as effective as possible.
