Module 1: Foundations

Part 1 - Accounting & Financial Statement Analysis

Module 1: Foundations

Core Accounting Principles

  • GAAP vs. IFRS: Rules-based (US) vs. Principles-based (Intl). Goal: Transparency, Consistency, Comparability.
  • Historical Context: Post-1929 crash response, aimed to mitigate information asymmetry.
  • Key GAAP Principles: Prudence (Conservatism) - recognize expenses/liabilities ASAP, revenues/assets when certain. Also Going Concern, Materiality, Full Disclosure.
  • Accrual Method: Recognize revenue when earned, expenses when incurred (matching principle). Critical for true economic picture.
Module 1: Accounting & FSA

Deconstructing the Three Financial Statements

  • Balance Sheet: Snapshot (Assets = Liabilities + Equity). Assets (liquidity order), Liabilities (immediacy order), Equity (residual interest).
  • Income Statement (P&L): Performance over period. Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → OpEx → Operating Income (EBIT) → Net Income.
  • Statement of Cash Flows: Tracks actual cash movement. CFO (core business), CFI (long-term assets), CFF (owners/creditors). Often called the "truth-teller."
Module 1: Accounting & FSA

Interconnectivity & Revenue Recognition

  • Key Links: Net Income → CFO & Retained Earnings. Working Capital changes → CFO. CapEx → CFI & BS PP&E. Debt/Equity changes → CFF & BS. Ending Cash (CFS) = Cash (BS).
  • Revenue Recognition (ASC 606): 5-step model (Identify contract, performance obligations, transaction price, allocate price, recognize when/as satisfied). Crucial for assessing earnings quality.
  • Expense Analysis: COGS vs. OpEx (SG&A, R&D, D&A). Analyze trends for margin insights.
  • Importance of Footnotes: Critical for full understanding; detail accounting policies, debt, commitments, contingencies. Red flags: frequent accounting changes, opaque language.
Module 1: Accounting & FSA

Part 2 - Financial Analysis & Modeling

Module 1: Financial Analysis & Modeling

Building the 3-Statement Financial Model

  • Purpose: Forecast performance, test assumptions, assess debt service capacity.
  • Best Practices: Clear structure (Inputs, Historicals, Projections, Schedules), Color-coding, Periodicity, Explicit formulas, Checks (Balance Sheet balances!).
  • Historical Data: 3-5 years minimum for trends, seasonality.
  • Supporting Schedules: Debt, PP&E (CapEx/Depreciation), Working Capital (DSO, DIH, DPO), Equity, Tax. These are the "engine room."
Module 1: Financial Analysis & Modeling

Forecasting & Key Credit Ratios

  • Forecasting Order: Income Statement → Balance Sheet (operational items) → Cash Flow Statement (derived) → Balance Model (Cash/Revolver as plug). Watch for circular references!
  • Leverage Ratios: Debt/EBITDA, Net Debt/EBITDA, Debt/Capital. (Measure debt burden).
  • Coverage Ratios: Interest Coverage (EBITDA/Interest), DSCR, FCCR. (Measure ability to pay debt service).
  • Liquidity Ratios: Current Ratio, Quick Ratio. (Measure short-term solvency).
  • Profitability Ratios: Gross/Operating/Net Margins, ROA, ROE. (Support debt repayment).
Module 1: Financial Analysis & Modeling

Advanced Analysis Techniques

  • Sensitivity Analysis: Change *one* assumption (e.g., revenue growth, margins) to see impact on key outputs (ratios, cash flow). Identifies key risk drivers.
  • Scenario Analysis: Change *multiple* assumptions for plausible scenarios (Base Case, Upside Case, Downside/Stress Case). Tests resilience of financial projections.
  • Stress Testing: A more extreme form of scenario analysis focusing on severe but plausible crisis events to gauge borrower survivability.

Module 2: Understanding the Market

Part 1 - Capital Markets & Leveraged Loans

Module 2: Market Understanding

The Capital Ecosystem

  • Primary Markets: New securities issued (IPOs, new bonds/loans). Proceeds to issuer. Underwriters (investment banks) facilitate.
  • Secondary Markets: Existing securities traded among investors (exchanges, OTC). Provides liquidity & price discovery.
  • Participants: Sell-Side (Investment banks), Buy-Side (Institutional investors).
Module 2: Capital Markets

The Spectrum of Debt (Capital Structure)

  • Senior Secured Debt: Highest claim, collateral-backed (Revolvers, Term Loans). Lowest risk/cost.
  • Senior Unsecured Debt: General claim, no specific collateral (Bonds/Notes, High-Yield Bonds).
  • Subordinated Debt (Junior): Ranks below senior debt (Sub. Bonds, Mezzanine).
  • Equity: Ownership, residual claim. Highest risk/potential return.
Module 2: Capital Markets

Leveraged Loans & Credit Agreements

  • Leveraged Loans: For highly levered/non-IG companies (LBOs, M&A). Higher risk/rates, secured, syndicated.
  • Credit Agreement: Defines terms. Key Sections: Definitions (EBITDA!), Loan Terms, Reps & Warranties, CPs, Covenants (Affirmative, Negative, Financial - Maintenance vs. Incurrence), EoDs.
  • Cov-Lite: Loans lacking financial maintenance covenants. Delays early warning.
Module 2: Capital Markets

Part 2 - Industry Analysis

Module 2: Industry Analysis

Importance & Porter's Five Forces

  • Why Industry Context: Shapes performance, projection realism, risk identification.
  • Porter's Five Forces: Framework for industry structure & profitability.
    • Threat of New Entrants (Barriers: capital, scale, brand).
    • Bargaining Power of Buyers (Concentration, differentiation).
    • Bargaining Power of Suppliers (Concentration, input criticality).
    • Threat of Substitutes (Alternatives outside industry).
    • Rivalry Among Existing Competitors (Number, growth, differentiation).
Module 2: Industry Analysis

Other Key Industry Considerations

  • Industry-Specific KPIs: e.g., Retail (SSS), Airlines (RASM), SaaS (ARR).
  • Broad Industry Risks: Cyclicality, Technological Disruption, Regulatory Risk, Geopolitical Factors, ESG Risks, Supply Chain Risks.
  • Other Frameworks: PESTLE Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental), SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats).

Module 3: The Analyst's Craft

Part 1 - Business Writing & Grammar

Module 3: Analyst's Craft

The Credit Memorandum: Purpose & Structure

  • Purpose: Primary written analysis; persuasive document for decision-makers. Clarity, conciseness, objectivity.
  • Standard Sections: Exec Summary (BLUF!), Borrower/Mgmt Overview, Industry Analysis, Financial Analysis, Repayment Sources, Loan Structure/Terms, Covenants, Conclusion.
Module 3: Business Writing

Effective Business Writing Principles

  • Direct (BLUF) & Active Voice. Be Specific & Data-Driven. Avoid Jargon.
  • Structure for Readability: Headings, short paragraphs, bullets. Conciseness.
  • Grammar & Style: Professionalism. Focus on agreements, clarity, consistency. Avoid common word confusion. Proofread!
Module 3: Business Writing

Module 3: The Analyst's Craft

Part 2 - Critical Thinking & Forming a Narrative

Module 3: Analyst's Craft

Essence of Critical Thinking in Credit

  • Disciplined Process: Conceptualizing, applying, analyzing, synthesizing, evaluating info.
  • Core Tenets: Objectivity, Thoroughness, Logical Reasoning, Clarity, Accuracy, Relevance, Healthy Skepticism.
  • Framework: Define Problem → Gather/Evaluate Info → Analyze/Interpret → Challenge Assumptions → Synthesize & Conclude → Communicate.
Module 3: Critical Thinking

Key Drivers, Risks, Mitigants & The Narrative

  • Identify Key Factors: Drivers (Strengths), Risks (Weaknesses), Mitigants. Balance of these drives decisions.
  • Beware Biases: Confirmation, Anchoring, Availability, Groupthink, Halo/Horn, Sunk Cost.
  • The Credit Narrative IS the Analysis: Weaves quantitative & qualitative into a coherent, persuasive story. Justifies recommendation. Answers "So What?".
Module 3: Critical Thinking

Module 4: The Credit Lifecycle

Part 1 - SNC & Underwriting New Issuers

Module 4: Credit Lifecycle

Shared National Credit (SNC) Program

  • Purpose: Consistent regulatory review of large syndicated loans ($100M+ & 3+ institutions) by FRS, FDIC, OCC. Monitors systemic risk.
  • SNC Classifications: Pass, Special Mention (SM - early warning), Classified (Substandard, Doubtful, Loss - repayment jeopardy).
  • Implications: Drives loan loss provisions, capital adequacy, risk management intensity.
Module 4: SNC & Underwriting

Underwriting: Process & Due Diligence

  • Process: Origination → Due Diligence → Credit Analysis/Memo → Committee Approval → Documentation → Closing/Funding.
  • Due Diligence: Financial (Historicals, QoE, Projections), Business/Commercial (Market, Competition, Mgmt), Legal/Regulatory.
  • 5 C's: Character, Capacity (Cash Flow), Capital, Collateral, Conditions.
Module 4: SNC & Underwriting

Module 4: The Credit Lifecycle

Part 2 - LBOs, M&A & Ongoing Monitoring

Module 4: Credit Lifecycle

LBOs & M&A Credit Analysis

  • LBOs (Creditor View): High leverage from PE. Risks: thin equity, high fixed charges. Modeling: debt capacity, cash flow sweep, covenants under stress.
  • M&A Analysis: Evaluate combined entity. Factors: Strategic Rationale, Financing Structure, Synergies (Cost vs. Revenue - realism is key), Integration Risk.
Module 4: LBOs, M&A, Monitoring

Ongoing Monitoring & Watchlist Management

  • Portfolio Management: Optimize risk-return, diversify, ensure compliance.
  • Early Warning Signs (EWS): Quantitative (declining ratios, neg CFO) & Qualitative (payment habits, mgmt issues, delayed financials).
  • Watchlist: For credits with increased risk. Process: updated analysis, frequent reporting, action plan (forbearance, restructure).

Module 5: The Future of Risk Management

AI and Emerging Trends in Credit Analysis

Module 5: Future of Risk

Transformation & Key Technologies

  • Shift: From reactive/historical to proactive, predictive, efficient. Drivers: data explosion, AI/ML advances.
  • Machine Learning (ML): Credit scoring (PD/LGD), classification. Integrates Alternative Data.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Sentiment analysis, info extraction (covenants), EWS from text.
Module 5: Future of Risk

AI in Action, Next Frontier & Challenges

  • AI Cases: Algorithmic risk mgmt, NPL reduction, GenAI for credit memo automation.
  • Next Frontier: AI in Portfolio Management, Intelligent Stress Testing, Adv. Model Risk Mgmt.
  • Challenges: "Black Box" (need XAI), Data Bias, Model Risk, Regulatory Uncertainty.
  • Evolving Analyst: "Human-in-the-loop" - oversee AI, critical questions, ethics.

Module 6: Advanced Topics

Part 1 - Advanced FSA & Quality of Earnings (QoE)

Module 6: Advanced Topics

Advanced FSA & Quality of Earnings (QoE)

  • Importance: Deeper scrutiny for complex companies, M&A, distress. QoE: sustainability, reliability of earnings.
  • Aggressive Accounting Red Flags: Rev Rec (premature, channel stuffing), Expenses (delaying, improper capitalization, under-reserving), Inventory (overstating), "Big Bath" charges, OBSF.
Module 6: Adv. FSA & QoE

Earnings Sustainability & Advanced Ratios

  • Normalizing Earnings: Adjust for non-recurring items, accounting differences, M&A. Goal: reflect ongoing earning capacity.
  • DuPont Analysis (ROE): `Profit Margin * Asset Turnover * Equity Multiplier`.
  • Advanced Cash Flow Ratios: CFO/Debt, FCF/Debt, Cash Interest Coverage. CCC. Altman Z-Score.
Module 6: Adv. FSA & QoE

Module 6: Advanced Topics

Part 2 - Debt Structuring & Restructuring

Module 6: Advanced Topics

Debt Structuring & Advanced Covenants

  • Structuring: Borrower vs. Lender objectives. Elements: Amount/Type, Seniority, Security, Tenor, Repayment, Pricing, Covenants.
  • Adv. Covenants: EBITDA Definition (critical!), Headroom, Equity Cure Rights, Baskets, Springing Covenants.
Module 6: Debt Structuring

Debt Restructuring & Chapter 11 Overview

  • Restructuring: When company can't meet debt. Goal: sustainable structure, viability, maximize recovery. Methods: Amend & Extend, Debt-for-Equity, Haircuts.
  • Out-of-Court vs. In-Court (Chapter 11): Workouts vs. Bankruptcy.
  • Ch. 11 Concepts: Automatic Stay, DIP, POR, Creditors' Committees, Cramdown, APR.
Module 6: Debt Restructuring

Part 3 - Legal Aspects of Loan Documentation

Module 6: Advanced Topics

Key Loan Documents & Agreement Clauses

  • Importance: Define rights, obligations, remedies. Docs: Loan Agmt, Notes, Security Agmts, Guarantees, Intercreditor Agmts.
  • Key Clauses: Reps & Warranties, Conditions Precedent, Events of Default (non-payment, covenant breach, MAC), Remedies.
Module 6: Legal Aspects

Security, Perfection, Guarantees & Intercreditor

  • Security Docs & Perfection: Security Agmt (personal property), Mortgage (real property). Perfection (UCC-1) makes lien effective vs third parties.
  • Guarantees: Third-party promise to pay debt. Enforceability issues (corp. benefit).
  • Intercreditor/Subordination Agreements: Define rights/priorities between creditor groups.

Module 7: Advanced Communication Skills

Persuasive Writing, Negotiation, and Effective Interaction

Module 7: Communication

Advanced Business Writing: Persuasion & Narrative

  • Persuasion: Build compelling, evidence-based case. Anticipate concerns.
  • Strong Narrative: Clear "thesis", strategic framing, balance objectivity, weave quant & qual.
  • Tailor to Audience: Credit Committee, Sr. Mgmt, RMs. Style: Precision, strong verbs.
Module 7: Communication

Internal & External Communication

  • Credit Committee: Know deal, anticipate questions, clear articulation, composure.
  • Collaborating with RMs: Understand RM view, clear on credit concerns, problem-solve.
  • Core Skills: Active Listening, Clarity (Verbal), Questioning, Constructive Feedback, Conflict Mgmt.
Module 7: Communication

Basic Negotiation Principles & Ethics

  • Preparation: Know objectives, facts, BATNA (Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement).
  • Focus on Interests, Not Positions. Separate People from Problem. Aim for Win-Win.
  • Ethical Communication: Honesty, transparency, confidentiality, fairness.

Module 8: Specialized Industry Analysis

Adapting Frameworks for Unique Sector Risks & KPIs

Module 8: Specialized Industries

Framework for Specialized Industries

  • Beyond General Analysis: Many industries need tailored approaches.
  • Identify Unique Traits & Tailor Frameworks: Adapt financial analysis, Porter's, risk assessment.
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Identify & interpret industry-specific KPIs.
Module 8: Specialized Industries

Case Study: Software as a Service (SaaS)

  • Overview: Subscription, cloud-hosted, scalable. High GM (mature), high upfront R&D/S&M.
  • Key SaaS KPIs: ARR/MRR, CAC, LTV (LTV/CAC > 3x), Churn (NRR > 100% strong), CAC Payback, Rule of 40.
  • Risks & Analysis: High churn, cash burn. Focus on recurring revenue quality, unit economics, FCF.

Module 9: Regulatory & Ethical Landscape

Compliance and Integrity in Credit Risk Management

Module 9: Regulatory & Ethics

Regulatory Landscape for Lending

  • Importance: Ensures safety/soundness, protects borrowers, prevents financial crime.
  • Key Frameworks: Capital Adequacy (Basel - RWA impact), Dodd-Frank (systemic risk), Fair Lending Laws (ECOA - non-discrimination).
Module 9: Regulatory & Ethics

AML, KYC, Data Privacy & Ethics

  • AML/KYC: Prevent illicit funds. Verify customer identity (entity & beneficial owners), understand business/fund sources, monitor activity. Analyst role in due diligence.
  • Data Privacy (GLBA, GDPR): Protect sensitive customer data. Adhere to security policies.
  • Internal Policies: Often more restrictive. Define underwriting standards, risk ratings. Analysts must know & adhere.
  • Ethical Conduct: Professional codes (integrity, objectivity), avoid conflicts, diligence, fair dealing.
Module 9: Regulatory & Ethics

Module 10: Quantitative Skills & Data Analytics

Tools and Techniques Beyond AI/ML

Module 10: Quant Skills

Role of Data Analytics & Tools

  • Enhances Credit Lifecycle: Underwriting (criteria refinement), Portfolio Monitoring (trends, EWS), Risk Modeling (PD/LGD/EAD), Reporting.
  • Tools (Conceptual):
    • Adv. Excel: PivotTables, Data Tables, Stat Functions, Power Query.
    • Python/R: Automation, complex transformations, statistical modeling (awareness level).
    • BI Tools (Tableau, Power BI): Interactive dashboards, visual exploration.
Module 10: Quant Skills

Data Visualization & Use Cases

  • Effective Visualization: Right chart type, clarity, highlight insights, audience awareness, data integrity.
  • Use Cases: Portfolio Segmentation (risk distribution), EWS from Data Trends (time series of ratios), Basic PD Modeling Concepts (Logistic Regression, Credit Scoring).
  • Limitations & Ethics: Data Quality ("GIGO"), Correlation vs. Causation, Overfitting, Model Risk. Ethical use: Bias, Privacy, Transparency.

Module 11: Capstone Project

Integrated Credit Analysis Application

Module 11: Capstone

Capstone Project: Overview & Objectives

  • Purpose: Culminating experience to integrate and apply all CRAFT module skills.
  • Role: Act as credit analyst assessing a company seeking financing (e.g., "Innovatech Solutions Group" case).
  • Objectives: Demonstrate proficiency in FSA, 3-statement modeling, industry analysis, critical thinking (risks/drivers/mitigants), underwriting process, and clear communication (memo & presentation outline).
Module 11: Capstone

Capstone Deliverables & Evaluation

  • Deliverable 1: 3-Statement Financial Model: Historicals & Projections, clear assumptions, supporting schedules, ratios, downside scenario. Must balance.
  • Deliverable 2: Comprehensive Credit Memorandum: Exec Summary, Borrower/Industry/Financial/Risk Analysis, Loan Structure, Repayment Sources, Recommendation.
  • Deliverable 3: Credit Committee Presentation Outline.
  • Evaluation: Analytical rigor, critical thinking, model quality, memo clarity/persuasiveness, thoroughness, professionalism, application of course concepts.

Appendix: General Learning Tips for Analysts

Curated Advice

  • Master fundamentals: Accounting, FSA, market context.

  • Develop strong writing skills: Clarity, conciseness, persuasive narrative.

  • Seek feedback & continuously learn: The field is dynamic.
