Part I: Foundations of Credit Risk

This part delves into the foundational concepts of credit risk, distinguishing between individual and portfolio-level assessments, and elaborates on the three pillars of Expected Loss (EL): Probability of Default (PD), Loss Given Default (LGD), and Exposure at Default (EAD).

Part II: The Qualitative Assessment: Understanding the Business and its Environment

This section outlines the qualitative analytical methodologies, including the timeless "5 C's of Credit" and a systematic approach to understanding the business and its environment.

Part III: The Quantitative Assessment: A Deep Dive into Financial Health

This part details the rating scales and methodologies of leading external agencies like S&P and Moody's, alongside considerations for specific industries.

Part IV: Structuring the Deal and Managing Ongoing Risk

This section provides practical tools such as due diligence questions, an understanding of loan and derivative agreement standards (LMA and ISDA), and advanced techniques like forecasting and stress testing.

Part V: The Thorough and Disciplined Credit Analyst

The overarching aim is to equip analysts with the ability to synthesize complex information, challenge assumptions, apply critical thinking, and formulate robust credit conclusions and appropriate ratings.
