Static Checklist for Rating Determination

This checklist is designed to help you think through the rating process in a step-by-step fashion, especially when dealing with limited information. Check the boxes that apply to the credit to get a sense of the most likely rating category.

PASS

A "Pass" rating indicates that the credit is fundamentally sound and that the borrower is expected to be able to repay the debt in a timely manner. The primary source of repayment is strong and reliable.

  • Strong and consistent cash flow from operations.
  • Low leverage (e.g., Debt/EBITDA well below industry norms).
  • Strong liquidity position (e.g., high current and quick ratios).
  • Well-defined and sustainable competitive advantages.
  • Experienced and credible management team.
  • Favorable industry trends and a strong market position.

Special Mention

A "Special Mention" rating indicates that the credit has potential weaknesses that deserve management's close attention. If left uncorrected, these potential weaknesses may result in deterioration of the repayment prospects for the asset or in the institution's credit position at some future date.

  • Declining trend in revenues or margins.
  • Increasing leverage or a tightening of liquidity.
  • Adverse industry trends or increased competition.
  • Management turnover or a change in strategy.
  • Covenant violations are possible in the near future.
  • The primary source of repayment is still intact, but there are concerns about its long-term sustainability.

Substandard

A "Substandard" rating indicates that the credit is inadequately protected by the current sound worth and paying capacity of the obligor or of the collateral pledged, if any. Assets so classified must have a well-defined weakness or weaknesses that jeopardize the liquidation of the debt. They are characterized by the distinct possibility that the institution will sustain some loss if the deficiencies are not corrected.

  • A well-defined weakness that jeopardizes repayment (e.g., a sustained period of operating losses).
  • The primary source of repayment is insufficient to service the debt.
  • The borrower is relying on secondary sources of repayment (e.g., asset sales, refinancing) to service the debt.
  • Covenant violations have occurred and have not been waived.
  • The institution has a high probability of sustaining a loss if the deficiencies are not corrected.

Non-Accrual

A non-accrual asset is a loan where the accrual of interest has been suspended because the borrower has failed to make interest or principal payments for a specified period of time. A loan is typically placed on non-accrual status when it is 90 days or more past due.

  • The loan is 90 days or more past due on interest or principal payments.
  • The borrower is in bankruptcy or a similar proceeding.
  • The institution has determined that the collection of interest is not probable.
