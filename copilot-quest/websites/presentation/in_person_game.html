Round 1: Risk Management

Question 1: What is the primary goal of risk management?

Answer: To identify, assess, and mitigate risks.

Anecdote: A famous example of risk management failure is the story of the Titanic. The ship's designers knew about the risk of icebergs, but they didn't take it seriously enough. As a result, the ship sank on its maiden voyage, and more than 1,500 people died.

Round 1: Risk Management

Question 2: What is a risk matrix?

Answer: A tool for ranking and prioritizing risks.

Anecdote: A risk matrix is like a weather forecast for your business. It helps you see which storms are coming and how to prepare for them.

Round 2: Compliance

Question 1: What is the purpose of a compliance program?

Answer: To ensure that an organization follows all applicable laws and regulations.

Anecdote: In the 1970s, the Ford Pinto was known for its tendency to explode in rear-end collisions. Ford knew about the problem, but they decided not to fix it because it would have been too expensive. As a result, hundreds of people were killed or injured. This is a classic example of what can happen when a company doesn't take compliance seriously.

Round 2: Compliance

Question 2: What are some of the consequences of non-compliance?

Answer: Fines, penalties, and reputational damage.

Anecdote: In 2015, Volkswagen was caught cheating on emissions tests. The company was fined billions of dollars, and its reputation was tarnished. This is a reminder that non-compliance can have serious consequences.

Round 3: Safety

Question 1: What is the most common cause of workplace accidents?

Answer: Slips, trips, and falls.

Anecdote: A few years ago, a man in Florida was killed when he fell into a vat of chocolate. This is a tragic reminder that even the most seemingly harmless workplaces can be dangerous.

Round 3: Safety

Question 2: What is the purpose of a safety data sheet (SDS)?

Answer: To provide information on the hazards of a chemical and how to handle it safely.

Anecdote: In 2013, a fertilizer plant in West, Texas, exploded, killing 15 people and injuring more than 160. The explosion was caused by ammonium nitrate, a chemical that is used in fertilizer. The plant didn't have a proper SDS for ammonium nitrate, so workers didn't know how to handle it safely.
