Risks & Management Interview

The financial data only tells half the story. The following transcript from the management interview provides critical context for the FY23 underperformance. Pay close attention to management's explanations and the associated analyst red flags.

Transcript: Apex Logistics Management Interview

Sarah (Analyst):

Thanks for joining. Looking at the 2023 numbers, we see a significant contraction in EBITDA margins—down to roughly 8% from 18% the prior year. Can you walk us through the drivers?

Alex (CEO):

Sure. It was a perfect storm. First, fuel prices spiked in Q2, and our hedging contracts rolled off in Q1. We decided to float, betting prices would stabilize. They didn't. That hit COGS hard. Second, we lost the *TechStream* contract.

Analyst Red Flag (Operational Risk): Management admits to a failed hedging strategy, exposing the business to commodity price volatility.

Sarah (Analyst):

TechStream? I didn't see that disclosed in the CIM. How much of revenue did they represent?

Alex (CEO):

About 18% of FY22 revenue. They moved their logistics in-house. It left us with a lot of idle trucks and warehouse space we’re still paying leases on.

Analyst Red Flag (Customer Concentration): The loss of a single customer (18% of revenue) was a primary driver of the performance decline. This risk was not disclosed in the initial materials.

Sarah (Analyst):

Understood. I see AR days have stretched from 60 to nearly 82 days. Is this a collection issue?

David (CFO):

Well, we're trying to pivot to new customers in the retail sector. To win that business, we had to offer generous payment terms—Net 90 in some cases. It’s a growth strategy, but it burns cash in the short term. That's why the Revolver is drawn.

Analyst Red Flag (Working Capital): The "growth strategy" is cash-incinerating. They are trading cash for revenue by extending terms, leading to the $13M revolver draw (from $2M).

Sarah (Analyst):

Speaking of growth, I see a $15M add-back to EBITDA for "Restructuring and Synergies." That’s larger than your actual EBITDA.

David (CFO):

Yes, we’re closing two regional hubs. We expect $10M in savings next year. The other $5M is... well, it’s projected savings from a new IT system we haven't fully implemented yet. We feel comfortable treating that as pro-forma EBITDA.

Analyst Red Flag (Aggressive Add-Backs): This is the core issue. They are reporting $14M of EBITDA but asking for credit based on $29M ($14M + $15M). The $5M for an "unimplemented" system is purely fictional and should be rejected by any creditor.

Sarah (Analyst):

One last question on the Term Sheet. You're asking for a leverage covenant of 6.5x. Given you're currently running at... well, significantly higher than that on a reported basis, how do you plan to stay compliant?

Alex (CEO):

If you include the add-backs, we're at 4.5x. We feel 6.5x gives us plenty of breathing room. Plus, the bank assured us they'd be "flexible" on the definition of EBITDA.

Structural Review & Term Sheet Analysis

A credit decision isn't just about the company's performance; it's about the legal protections in the loan agreement. A good company with a bad structure can be a terrible investment. Below are key (and problematic) excerpts from the proposed term sheet.

Facilities & Pricing

  • **Revolving Credit Facility:** $25,000,000 (SOFR + 350 bps)
  • **Term Loan B:** $50,000,000 (SOFR + 450 bps)

Analyst Comment: Pricing seems standard, but note the *No SOFR floor*. If rates fall, our yield falls. A 0.50% or 1.00% floor would be preferable.

Financial Covenants

  • **Total Net Leverage Ratio:** Shall not exceed **8.50x**.
  • **Interest Coverage Ratio:** Minimum of 1.50x.

FATAL FLAW (Structural Risk): An 8.50x leverage covenant is extraordinarily wide ("cov-lite"). The company's reported leverage is 6.14x. This covenant provides no real protection and would only be triggered long after the company is operationally insolvent. Market standard is closer to 5.0x-5.5x.

Negative Covenants

  • **Indebtedness:** Borrower may incur unlimited additional unsecured debt...
  • **Restricted Payments:** Borrower may pay dividends to equity holders using 100% of Excess Cash Flow.

FATAL FLAW (Structural Risk): The Restricted Payments (RP) clause is a major issue. It allows the private equity sponsor to dividend cash out of the company *before* paying down debt. In a turnaround, 100% of cash flow should be used to repay debt (a "cash flow sweep").

Events of Default

  • Non-payment of principal or interest.
  • Bankruptcy.

Analyst Comment (Structural Risk): The *absence* of a "Cross-Default" clause is a red flag. This means the company could default on its *other* debts (e.g., unsecured notes) and our loan would *not* be in default, preventing us from taking action.

EBITDA Definition

  • ...*Plus:* Any cost savings projected by the Borrower in good faith to be realized within 24 months.
  • ...*Plus:* All management fees paid to the Private Equity Sponsor (uncapped).

Analyst Comment (Structural Risk): This definition legally allows the questionable add-backs. The 24-month timeframe for synergies is too long (12 months is standard), and allowing "uncapped" management fees to be added back is a non-starter.
